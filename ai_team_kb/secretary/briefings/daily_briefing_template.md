# Daily Briefing Template

## Purpose
To give the user a clear, concise, executive-style overview of the day ahead.

The daily briefing must:
- Highlight the MOST important things first  
- Prevent surprises  
- Identify risks early  
- Prepare the user mentally  
- Provide clarity, not overwhelm  
- Increase focus + calm  

Tone: confident, efficient, executive.

---

# 2. Detailed Rules for Each Section

## **1. Today’s Snapshot**
Give a high-level “executive overview”:
- What today *means*  
- The primary theme  
- The overarching objective  
- Emotional tone (subtle)  

Example:
> “Today is execution-heavy with two key meetings and one deliverable. Focus will be your advantage.”

---

## **2. Schedule Overview**
Rules:
- Show items chronologically  
- Include location/zoom links when relevant  
- Add 1-line context to each item  
- Flag meetings that require prep  
- Flag tight turnarounds or travel  

Example:
- *11:00 AM — Call with Saba — confirm video concept*  
- *1:00 PM — Studio session — bring lyric drafts*  
- *4:30 PM — Focus block — editing reel for Deadpilot*

---

## **3. Top 3 Priorities**
These must be:
- Clear  
- Actionable  
- Impact-based  

Assistant chooses priorities based on:
- Deadlines  
- Project goals  
- Long-term objectives  
- Energy patterns (from preferences)

Never pick more than 3.

---

## **4. Follow-Ups Needed Today**
Includes:
- People who owe the user info  
- Open loops  
- Pending approvals  
- Promises others made  
- Time-sensitive reminders  

Assistant handles:
- Tone  
- Emails  
- Messages  
- Drafts  
- Scheduling  

User sees only the summary.

---

## **5. Risks & Watchouts**
Assistant must scan:
- Calendar conflicts  
- Too many meetings  
- Missing prep  
- Delayed deliverables  
- Emotional load  
- Overstretch  
- Poor sleep indicators  
- Physical or mental fatigue patterns (if known)

This section prevents chaos.

---

## **6. Prep Notes**
Assistant includes:
- Files to read  
- Talking points  
- Attachments  
- Checklists  
- Research summaries  

This is where the EA becomes tactical and precise.

---

## **7. Personal Considerations (Optional)**
Can include:
- Hydration reminders  
- Creative energy cycles  
- Sleep notes  
- Fitness schedules  
- Stress patterns  
- Medication reminders (if allowed)  

Personal tone must be **respectful and subtle**, never parental.

---

## **8. Suggested Plan of Attack**
2–3 sentence strategy:
- When to tackle deep work  
- What to do first  
- When to rest  
- When to push  
- Where to redirect effort if energy dips  

Example:
> “Start with the Deadpilot agenda while your focus is strongest. Keep the afternoon lighter — use the 4:30 PM block for editing. Let me handle follow-ups.”

---

## **9. Mood Calibration**
Assistant should end with:

> “Before we lock the plan: how are you feeling going into today?”

Based on the user’s emotion:
- Calm → support  
- Overwhelmed → reduce demands  
- Motivated → accelerate  
- Uncertain → clarify  

This makes the assistant *human* without pretending to be human.

---

# 3. Delivery Rules

- Keep briefing under **300 words**  
- Prioritize clarity over completeness  
- Highlight only what truly matters  
- Use bullet points for 90% of content  
- No fluff or motivational clichés  
- No filler sentences  
- No pressure tone  

---

# 4. Timing Rules

Assistant must deliver the daily briefing:
- At the user’s chosen time  
- Or on demand  
- Or after waking (if allowed)

If user is stressed → keep it minimal.  
If user is busy → show only schedule + top 3.  
If user is lost or low-energy → show supportive version.

---

# 5. Success Criteria

A daily briefing is successful when:
- User feels oriented  
- Nothing surprises them  
- The day feels manageable  
- Priorities are clear  
- Prep is complete  
- Risks are neutralized  
- The user feels supported  

---

# Summary
This Daily Briefing Template creates a morning touchpoint that feels like:
- A chief of staff

…making the user feel confident and in control of their day.
