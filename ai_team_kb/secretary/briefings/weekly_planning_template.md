# Weekly Planning Template

## Purpose
To give the user a high-level, strategic overview of the upcoming week while establishing priorities, protecting focus, and preventing overwhelm.

This weekly plan:
- Sets the tone and direction  
- Aligns tasks with long-term goals  
- Identifies risks early  
- Ensures deadlines are visible  
- Helps the user work intentionally  
- Balances ambition with reality  

Tone: strategic, calm, grounded, executive.

# 2. Explanation of Each Section

## **1. Theme of the Week**
A guiding idea based on:
- Deadlines  
- Workload  
- User goals  
- Emotional tone  

Examples:
- “Execution and progress.”  
- “Preparation and alignment.”  
- “Creativity and exploration.”  
- “Recovery and focus.”

---

## **2. Major Goals (Top 3)**
These must be:
- Outcome-based, not tasks  
- High-level  
- Tied to long-term goals  

Examples:
- “Finish draft of new single.”  
- “Launch product mockups.”  
- “Clear email backlog.”  
- “Record vocals for Deadpilot.”  

Never more than 3.

---

## **3. Key Deadlines**
Assistant must surface:
- Hard deadlines  
- Soft deadlines  
- Hidden deadlines (things that require prep)  
- Dependent deadlines from others  

This prevents surprises.

---

## **4. Important Events & Meetings**
Rules:
- Include only impactful events  
- Add 1-line context to each  
- Flag anything requiring preparation  

---

## **5. Project Breakdown**
Checklist for each active project:
- Objective (the week’s purpose)  
- Tasks  
- Risks  
- Mitigation  
- Next steps  

Keep it short and high-level.

---

## **6. Follow-Ups**
A weekly map of:
- People who may delay  
- Pending approvals  
- Promises from others  
- Items that could block progress  

Assistant handles communication automatically.

---

## **7. Risk Map**
Scan for:
- Overbooked days  
- Missing prep  
- External dependencies  
- Emotional + energy constraints  
- Travel  
- Bandwidth issues  
- Deadline clustering  

Assistant *must* offer mitigation strategies.

---

## **8. Recommended Focus Blocks**
These are scheduled based on:
- User’s energy preferences (from calendar_preferences.md)  
- Deadlines  
- Type of work (creative, admin, deep work)  

Examples:
- **Monday Morning:** “Deep work block — writing or strategy.”  
- **Wednesday Afternoon:** “Creative work — editing, mixing, visuals.”  
- **Friday:** “Admin — inbox zero + planning.”  

---

## **9. Personal & Energy Considerations**
Optional, but powerful.

Includes:
- Sleep issues  
- Stress  
- Hormonal cycles  
- Health reminders  
- Hydration  
- Overload risk  
- Creative surges  
- Recovery times  

Always gentle, never parental.

---

## **10. Suggested Weekly Strategy**
The assistant provides a simple 3–5 sentence game plan:

Example:
> “This week is all about clearing bottlenecks early so the back half is lighter. Front-load creative tasks into Monday–Tuesday. Use Wednesday as a pivot day for meetings. Keep Thursday open for deep work and Friday for cleanup.”

Tone: strong but calm.

---

## **11. Reflection Questions**
Optional section for user engagement.

Assistant may ask:
- “What would make this week successful?”  
- “What’s the one thing you absolutely want to get done?”  
- “Is there something we should say no to this week?”  

These questions help tailor next week’s planning.

---

# 3. Delivery Rules

- Delivered on a consistent weekly schedule  
- Must be sent **Sunday evening** or **Monday morning** unless user chooses otherwise  
- Must be under 500 words  
- Prioritize clarity  
- Do not overwhelm  
- Always provide a recommended weekly strategy  

---

# 4. Success Criteria

The weekly planning is successful when:
- User understands their week at a glance  
- Nothing sneaks up on them  
- Priorities are clear  
- Workload feels manageable  
- Risks are anticipated  
- The week feels strategic, not chaotic  

---

# Summary
A weekly briefing is a **high-level strategy session**.  
It aligns the user’s time, energy, and goals — creating a week that feels intentional, controlled, and meaningful.

This file trains the assistant to deliver that experience consistently.
