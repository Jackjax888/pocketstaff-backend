# Meeting Types & Definitions

## Purpose
To provide clear, standardized definitions of common meeting types.  
This allows the assistant to:
- Prepare properly  
- Apply the correct workflow  
- Understand context  
- Know what documents or prep are required  
- Estimate correct duration  
- Schedule meetings intelligently  

---

## 1. 1:1 Check-In
**Purpose:** Relationship building, updates, support, alignment  
**Typical Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Review past action items  
- Gather quick status updates  
- No deep documentation needed  

---

## 2. Internal Team Sync
**Purpose:** Status updates, alignment, sharing progress  
**Typical Duration:** 30–45 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Notes from last meeting  
- Task list or project tracker  
- Agenda with discussion items  

---

## 3. Client Meeting / Client Call
**Purpose:** Service, support, updates, problem-solving  
**Typical Duration:** 30–60 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Client history  
- Open issues  
- Documents or proposals  
- Questions to ask  

---

## 4. Sales Call / Demo
**Purpose:** Pitching, presenting, closing deals  
**Typical Duration:** 30–60 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Deck or demo materials  
- Research on client  
- Pricing sheets  
- Key talking points  

---

## 5. Strategy / Planning Session
**Purpose:** High-level thinking, roadmaps, decisions  
**Typical Duration:** 60–120 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Roadmaps  
- Goals  
- SWOT notes  
- Data summaries  

---

## 6. Creative Brainstorm
**Purpose:** Generate ideas, discuss concepts, explore directions  
**Typical Duration:** 45–90 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Moodboards or reference materials  
- Open questions  
- Creative prompts  

---

## 7. Interview (Hiring)
**Purpose:** Evaluate candidates  
**Typical Duration:** 30–60 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Candidate resume  
- Role description  
- Pre-written interview questions  

---

## 8. Vendor or Partner Meeting
**Purpose:** Negotiation, contracting, collaboration  
**Typical Duration:** 30–60 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Proposal  
- Pricing  
- Contract draft  
- Key concerns  

---

## 9. Performance Review
**Purpose:** Assess employee performance  
**Typical Duration:** 45–60 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Performance metrics  
- Notes from past 6–12 months  
- Goals for next period  

---

## 10. Project Kickoff
**Purpose:** Start a new project; define goals  
**Typical Duration:** 30–60 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Project brief  
- Timeline  
- Team roles  

---

## 11. Stand-Up / Daily Sync
**Purpose:** Quick updates and next steps  
**Typical Duration:** 10–15 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Nothing major  
- Yesterday/Today blockers list  

---

## 12. Review Meeting (Design, Product, Financial)
**Purpose:** Evaluate work or results  
**Typical Duration:** 45–60 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Design files  
- Reports or data  
- Feedback list  

---

## 13. Board Meeting
**Purpose:** High-level strategy, governance  
**Typical Duration:** 60–180 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Board deck  
- Financials  
- Agenda  
- Notes from last meeting  

---

## 14. Investor Meeting
**Purpose:** Fundraising, reporting, relationship building  
**Typical Duration:** 45–60 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Pitch deck  
- Metrics  
- Milestones  
- Upcoming goals  

---

## 15. Crisis / Emergency Meeting
**Purpose:** Rapid decision-making  
**Typical Duration:** 15–30 minutes  
**Prep Needed:**  
- Clear summary of issue  
- Proposed solutions  
- Needed decisions  

---

## Summary for the Assistant
Each meeting type signals:
- **How much prep is required**  
- **What documents are needed**  
- **How long the meeting should be**  
- **Whether the user needs extra briefing**  
- **Where to schedule it in the Ideal Week**

The assistant uses this dictionary to apply the correct workflows every time.
