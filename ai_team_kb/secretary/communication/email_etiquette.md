# Email Etiquette Training

## Purpose
To teach the assistant how to write clear, polite, and professional emails that represent the user with accuracy and respect.

## Core Principles
1. Clarity first — make the message easy to understand.
2. Respect the recipient's time — keep it concise.
3. Professional tone — polite, neutral, confident.
4. Accuracy — double-check names, dates, and details.
5. Purpose — every email should have a clear reason and next step.

---

## Standard Email Structure

1. **Subject Line**
   - Short, specific, and scannable.
   - Examples:
     - `Meeting Request – [Topic] – [Date]`
     - `Follow-Up – [Topic]`
     - `[Action Required] Review by [Date]`

2. **Greeting**
   - Default: “Hi [Name],”
   - Formal: “Dear [Name],”

3. **Opening Sentence**
   - State purpose immediately.
   - Examples:
     - “I’m reaching out to schedule…”
     - “I’m writing on behalf of [Executive Name]…”

4. **Body**
   - One idea per paragraph.
   - Use bullet points for options, dates, or steps.

5. **Call to Action**
   - Clear statement of what you need.
   - Add deadlines or confirmation requests when appropriate.

6. **Closing**
   - “Best regards,”
   - “Kind regards,”
   - “Sincerely,”

7. **Signature**
   - Name  
   - Title  
   - Company  
   - Contact info  

---

## Tone Rules

### Default Tone
- Polite  
- Neutral  
- Clear  
- Confident  
- No slang, no emojis, no excessive exclamation marks  

### Formal Tone
Use for clients, executives, and unfamiliar contacts.
- Avoid contractions  
- Use complete sentences  
- Maintain distance and respect  

### Semi-Formal Tone
Use for colleagues and familiar partners.
- Contractions okay  
- Warm but still professional  

### Internal Notes Tone
- Shorter, but still polite  
- No abrupt or blunt one-line messages

## Common Scenarios & Templates

### Scheduling a Meeting (External)

**Subject:** `Meeting Request – [Topic] with [Executive Name]`

**Body:**

Dear [Name],  
I’m writing on behalf of [Executive Name] to schedule a [duration] meeting regarding [topic].

Could you please let us know if any of the following times work for you (in [time zone])?

- [Option 1 – Date, Time]  
- [Option 2 – Date, Time]  
- [Option 3 – Date, Time]  

If none of these are convenient, we’re happy to consider alternatives.

Kind regards,  
[Your Name]  
Executive Assistant to [Executive Name]  
[Company]  
[Email] | [Phone]

---

### Follow-Up (No Response)

**Subject:** `Follow-Up – [Topic]`

Hi [Name],  
I hope you’re well. I wanted to follow up on my message from [date] regarding [topic].  
When you have a moment, could you please share an update or availability?

Thank you,  
[Your Name]

---

### Meeting Confirmation

**Subject:** `Confirmation – [Meeting Topic] on [Date]`

Hi [Name],  
This confirms your meeting with [Executive Name]:

- **Date:** [Date]  
- **Time:** [Time] ([time zone])  
- **Duration:** [Duration]  
- **Location / Link:** [Address or link]  
- **Participants:** [Names]  

Please let me know if any adjustments are needed.

Best regards,  
[Your Name]

---

### Sending Documents

**Subject:** `[Document/Report Name] Attached`

Hi [Name],  
Attached is the [document/report] you requested.  
If you need revisions or additional formats, feel free to let me know.

Best,  
[Your Name]

---

### Declining Politely

**Subject:** `Re: [Topic]`

Hi [Name],  
Thank you for reaching out. Unfortunately, [Executive Name] is unavailable during that time.  
However, we can offer one of the following alternatives:

- [Option 1]  
- [Option 2]  

Please let me know what works best.

Kind regards,  
[Your Name]

---

## Quality Checklist (Always Follow)

- Subject line is clear  
- Correct greeting and spelling  
- Purpose stated in first sentence  
- Bullets used for multiple items  
- Dates/times/links verified  
- Tone appropriate for audience  
- Signature included  
- Proofread before sending
